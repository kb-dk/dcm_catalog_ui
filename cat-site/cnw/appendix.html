D. Doubtful and spurious compositions

Compositions erroneously ascribed to Nielsen, autographs containing compositions of unknown origin and compositions mentioned in the literature, but for which evidence seems insufficient.

  • Piano piece, or more likely, an arrangement for piano of a song by Nielsen or another composer. 16 bars, F major, 3/4, no vocal text (DK-KkDet Kongelige Bibliotek, København CNSBirgit Bjørnum & Klaus Møllerhøj: Carl Nielsens Samling. Katalog over komponistens musikhåndskrifter i Det kongelige Bibliotek / The Carl Nielsen Collection. A Catalogue of the Composer's Musical Manuscripts in the Royal Library. The Royal Library: Copenhagen 1992. 426 (add.))
  • 'Det som lysner over Vangen'
    Listed as a privately owned manuscript (piano notation with text) as FS 160 in Dan Fog, Carl Nielsen. Kompositioner (Copenhagen 1965), according to whom it is Nielsen's final composition dated '23.IX.1931'. The song has also been ascribed to Nielsen in Torben Meyer & Frede Schandorf Petersen, Carl Nielsen. Kunstneren og mennesket (Copenhagen 1947), vol. 2, pp. 334, 355 and 359.
    Information on this composition, however, is probably based only on a note in Nielsen's calendar on 23 September 1931 reading as follows: 'Einar Skovrup. Foredrag og Konsert / “Det som lysner over Vangen” / Kl 1 Komponistforeningen' (quot. CNBJohn Fellow (ed.): Carl Nielsen Brevudgaven. Multivers: Copenhagen 2005–2015. XI/793). The title seems to refer to the lecture and recital at Komponistforeningen, not to an unknown song by Nielsen. The text 'Det, som lysner over Vangen', Sang til Vallekilde [Højskole] of 1876, is by Jens C. Hostrup (1818–1892), not Frederik Poulsen as stated in FS.
  • Allegro for piano (manuscript, DK-KkDet Kongelige Bibliotek, København CNSBirgit Bjørnum & Klaus Møllerhøj: Carl Nielsens Samling. Katalog over komponistens musikhåndskrifter i Det kongelige Bibliotek / The Carl Nielsen Collection. A Catalogue of the Composer's Musical Manuscripts in the Royal Library. The Royal Library: Copenhagen 1992. 352a, gathering 26)
    Nielsen has written 'Allegro' at the top of the page while the rest is presumably in his daughter Irmelin's hand.
  • Nisserne ('De Nisser de pusler i Lo og i Lade')
    Canon notated from memory by Margrete Rosenberg, 1950; reproduced in Emilie Demant Hatt, Foraarsbølger, ed. John Fellow, Copenhagen 2002, p. 39.
  • Skomagerens Brudevals
    Reproduced in Torben Meyer & Frede Schandorff, Carl Nielsen. Kunstneren og Mennesket (Copenhagen 1947), p. 32. According to Nielsen's childhood friend, Jørgen Jul. Hansen, this waltz was composed by Nielsen with the assistance of his father, Niels Jørgensen (Niels Maler).
